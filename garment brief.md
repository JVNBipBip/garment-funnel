# Garment System — Landing Page Build Brief

> **Purpose:** This document is a complete brief for a coding agent to build a high-converting landing page that sells blank apparel sample packs and drives bulk orders for Garment System (garmentsystem.com). Every section, piece of copy direction, and design note is informed by deep market research on the blank apparel industry.

---

## 1. BUSINESS CONTEXT

**What Garment System does:** Premium blank apparel supplier + custom cut-and-sew manufacturer with a digital design platform. Based in Montreal, Canada. Ships to CA, US, UK, AU, NZ.

**What makes them unique (no competitor offers all of these):**
- Premium blank garments (heavyweight tees, hoodies, joggers, etc.)
- Custom cut-and-sew manufacturing at low MOQs
- Digital design/customization platform
- 3-day sample turnaround
- Sample pack → bulk order pipeline

**Primary conversion goal:** Get visitors to order a Sample Pack ($29–$39 range).
**Secondary conversion goal:** Get sample pack buyers to place a Bulk Order.

**Target audiences (in priority order):**
1. Emerging streetwear / fashion brand founders (0–2 years in business, doing <$50K/year)
2. Established brands scaling production (2+ years, looking to switch from Gildan/Bella+Canvas to premium or custom)
3. Merch companies / content creators wanting branded blanks
4. Print-on-demand businesses upgrading to premium blanks
5. Athleisure and fitness brands

**Geographic targets:** Canada (home market advantage), USA, United Kingdom, Australia, New Zealand.

## 3. TOP 10 CUSTOMER PAIN POINTS (with copy direction)

These are the exact frustrations brand owners express on Reddit (r/streetwearstartup, r/clothingbrand, r/Entrepreneur), Trustpilot, and industry forums. **The landing page must address every single one.**

### Pain Point #1: MOQs are too high
**The problem:** Most manufacturers require 300–1,000 pieces per style per color. Startups can't afford that. They need 50–100 to test the market.
**How Garment System solves it:** Low/no minimum order quantities. Sample packs available.
**Copy direction:** "No 500-unit minimums. Start with a sample pack. Scale when you're ready."

### Pain Point #2: Quality inconsistency between batches
**The problem:** Sample looks great, bulk order shows up lighter weight, different texture, sizing is off. Brands lose trust with their own customers.
**How Garment System solves it:** Consistent quality control, same production line for samples and bulk.
**Copy direction:** "What you sample is what you get. Every batch. Guaranteed." — Include GSM specs, wash-test data, before/after photos.

### Pain Point #3: Shipping delays destroy launch timelines
**The problem:** Brands pre-sell drops and rely on delivery dates. Suppliers ship late with zero communication. Lost sales, damaged reputation.
**How Garment System solves it:** 3-day sampling. Clear production timelines. North American shipping (not overseas 60-day waits).
**Copy direction:** "3-day samples. Clear production timelines. Never miss a drop again."

### Pain Point #4: Suppliers ghost after taking payment
**The problem:** After payment, communication stops. Emails ignored, social media blocked. Extremely common with overseas manufacturers.
**How Garment System solves it:** Montreal-based company. Real people. Dedicated support.
**Copy direction:** "Real people in Montreal. Not a faceless factory overseas. We answer within 24 hours — always."

### Pain Point #5: Fear of scams and middlemen
**The problem:** First-time buyers are terrified. Alibaba middlemen, stolen deposits, bait-and-switch quality. The #1 conversion blocker.
**How Garment System solves it:** Verifiable Canadian company, physical address, video content of operations, risk-free sample pack.
**Copy direction:** "Visit our facility. Watch us work. See exactly where your garments are made." — Include factory footage, address, team photos.

### Pain Point #6: No pricing transparency
**The problem:** Competitors hide pricing behind account creation, phone calls, or "request a quote" forms. Brand owners want to see numbers before committing.
**How Garment System solves it:** Show pricing tiers on the page. Don't hide behind forms.
**Copy direction:** Display a visible pricing table with per-unit costs at different volume tiers (100+, 500+, 1,000+). This alone differentiates from every major competitor.

### Pain Point #7: Decision paralysis — blanks vs. custom
**The problem:** Brand owners don't know if they should buy blanks and print on them, or go custom cut-and-sew. They don't know when to transition.
**How Garment System solves it:** They offer both under one roof. Start with blanks, graduate to custom when ready.
**Copy direction:** "Start with blanks. Grow into custom. We're the only partner you need for both." — Include a visual "journey" showing the progression.

### Pain Point #8: Blanks feel cheap / hurt brand perception
**The problem:** Gildan-tier blanks feel thin, shrink, and don't hold prints well. Brands that use cheap blanks get called out on Reddit and TikTok.
**How Garment System solves it:** Premium heavyweight blanks (220–300+ GSM). The kind that streetwear brands charge $45+ for.
**Copy direction:** "The same heavyweight quality behind $80 retail tees — at wholesale prices." — Lead with GSM weight specs and fabric close-ups.

### Pain Point #9: Sustainability concerns
**The problem:** 60% of consumers consider sustainability when buying fashion. Eco-conscious brands need ethical supply chains.
**How Garment System solves it:** Highlight any certifications (OEKO-TEX, GOTS, WRAP), ethical manufacturing practices, fabric sourcing.
**Copy direction:** "Responsibly made. Ethically sourced. Because your customers care — and so do we."

### Pain Point #10: Private labeling and branding limitations
**The problem:** Brand owners want custom woven labels, hang tags, branded packaging. Many blank suppliers don't offer these services.
**How Garment System solves it:** Full private labeling, custom tags, branded packaging available.
**Copy direction:** "Your brand, front to back. Custom labels, tags, and packaging included." — Show close-up photos of custom label options.

## 4. LANDING PAGE ARCHITECTURE (section-by-section)

Build the page in this exact order. Each section has a specific psychological purpose.

### SECTION 1: Hero (Above the Fold)
**Purpose:** Answer 4 questions instantly: Is this quality? How much? Is it risky? Is this legit?

**Elements:**
- **Background:** Auto-playing muted video loop OR high-impact hero image showing fabric texture, folded premium blanks, or hands examining garment quality
- **Headline (H1):** Outcome-focused, speaks to aspiration. Examples:
  - "Premium Blanks That Make Your Brand Look Like a Million Bucks"
  - "The Blanks Behind the Brands You Wish You Started"
  - "Stop Settling for Gildan. Start Building a Real Brand."
- **Subheadline:** Specificity-driven. Reference fabric weight, target audience, key differentiator. Example: "Heavyweight blanks (220–300 GSM) + custom cut-and-sew manufacturing. 3-day samples. No minimums."
- **Primary CTA button:** "Order Your Sample Pack — $29" (include price in the button)
- **Secondary CTA:** "Get a Bulk Quote" (text link or ghost button)
- **Trust badges strip** (directly below CTAs): "✓ No MOQ | ✓ 3-Day Samples | ✓ Free Shipping Over $X | ✓ Sample Cost Credited to Bulk Orders"
- **Social proof micro-element:** "Trusted by 500+ brands" or "Over X garments shipped" (small text near CTA)

**Design notes:**
- Dark or muted background to let garments pop
- CTA button should be high contrast (e.g., white or bright on dark)
- Hero video/image must show fabric texture — this is the single most important visual for this audience
- Mobile: Stack vertically, CTA must be visible without scrolling

## 6. DESIGN DIRECTION

### Visual Style
- **Premium, not corporate.** Think high-end streetwear lookbook meets clean SaaS landing page.
- **Dark mode base** with selective use of white/cream sections for contrast. This audience (streetwear, fashion founders) responds to dark, editorial aesthetics.
- Alternatively: Clean white base with dark accent sections — depends on brand guidelines.
- **Photography > illustrations.** Real fabric close-ups, real garments, real people. Zero stock photos.
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif. Something like Inter, Satoshi, or Neue Haas Grotesk. Bold weights for headlines, light for body.

### Color Palette (suggestions — adapt to Garment System's brand)
- Primary: Deep black (#0A0A0A) or navy (#1A1A2E)
- Accent: Warm white (#F5F5F0) or cream
- CTA: High-contrast pop color (orange, coral, or electric blue)
- Text: Off-white on dark, charcoal on light
- Success/trust elements: Muted green

### Image Requirements
- Hero: Fabric close-up or hands holding/examining blank garments (video preferred)
- Product shots: Clean flat-lays on neutral backgrounds
- Lifestyle: Finished branded products made from the blanks (aspiration)
- Behind-the-scenes: Montreal facility, production floor, quality check process
- Team: Real team photos (builds massive trust for this audience)

### Responsiveness
- Desktop: Full-width sections, side-by-side layouts
- Tablet: Adapt columns to single/double
- Mobile: Single column, sticky bottom CTA bar, thumb-friendly tap targets
- **Mobile-first design is critical** — 70%+ of this audience browses on mobile (Instagram/TikTok-driven traffic)

## 9. COPY TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

### Do:
- Write like a knowledgeable friend who runs their own brand, not like a corporation
- Use "you/your" constantly — make it about the brand owner, not about Garment System
- Be specific with numbers: GSM weights, shipping days, price per unit, response times
- Address fears directly: "We know you've been burned before. Here's why this is different."
- Use short sentences. Punch. Impact. Then explain.

### Don't:
- Use corporate jargon: "synergize," "leverage," "best-in-class," "solutions"
- Be vague: "high quality" means nothing without specs. Say "280 GSM combed ringspun cotton."
- Over-promise: Don't say "the best blanks on earth." Say "The same quality behind $80 retail tees."
- Use fake urgency: No countdown timers, no "only 2 left!" — this audience sees through it instantly
- Assume knowledge: Many visitors are first-time brand owners. Explain GSM, MOQ, cut-and-sew briefly where these terms appear.

### Voice examples:
- ❌ "We offer premium quality blank apparel solutions for businesses of all sizes"
- ✅ "Heavyweight blanks that make your brand feel like it belongs next to Fear of God on the rack"
- ❌ "Contact our sales team for a customized quote"
- ✅ "See bulk pricing right here, right now. No phone calls. No waiting."
- ❌ "Our garments undergo rigorous quality control processes"
- ✅ "Every batch is weight-tested, wash-tested, and size-checked before it ships. Period."
